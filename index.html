About Steel Titans

Our Journey

The Steel Titans are an FTC team from Claremont International High School in the Bronx, founded in September 2024. Now in our second year, we conduct most of our work at the NYC FIRST STEM Center at the Andrew Freedman House. As recently arrived immigrants, our team members have worked hard to learn, communicate, and succeed in a new environment. Most of us began with little to no prior experience in robotics, but through dedication and teamwork, we have embraced the challenge. This journey has been about more than just building a robot—it has been about developing confidence, leadership, and resilience. We are also part of a credit-bearing class at the NYC FIRST STEM Center at the Andrew Freedman House. In this program, we gain valuable technical and professional skills that support both our robotics work and future careers. We receive hands-on training with advanced tools and technologies, including CNC machines, laser cutters, Cricut machines, and 3D printers. We also learn 3D modeling and design, allowing us to create, prototype, and improve parts for our robot. Our hard work and perseverance have already led to strong results—we are currently ranked 13th out of 149 teams across New York City.

Connections

Stem connections

The Steel Titans actively engage with leaders in education, government, and the robotics industry to expand our knowledge and opportunities in STEM. We have connected with professionals such as Daniel Harris (Senior Executive Director, PSAL NYC DOE), who emphasized the importance of recognizing robotics and e-sports as competitive activities that require teamwork, strategy, and commitment. We also met with Dr. Larry D. Johnson, Jr., President of Bronx Community College, where we discussed the importance of expanding engineering pathways within the CUNY system and increasing access to STEM education. Through our work, we had the opportunity to speak with John B. King, Jr., Chancellor of SUNY and former U.S. Secretary of Education. We learned how government policy can support and expand access to engineering programs for students. Additionally, we visited Columbia University’s Engineering Department, where we explored the Makerspace lab and spoke with college students about their design processes. We also learned about student organizations such as the Columbia Space Initiative and CUFIRST. We also connected with Jon Miller Schwartz, Co-Founder and CEO of ULTRA Robotics, who shared his journey from FIRST Robotics Competition to leading a robotics company in New York City. He inspired us to use curiosity, hard work, and technical skills to pursue careers in STEM and entrepreneurship. Our team also participated in a special event with NYC Council Member Althea Stevens, who allocated $100,000 to the NYC FIRST STEM Center at the Andrew Freedman House. This funding expands access to robotics, engineering, and advanced technology opportunities for students across the Bronx. During the announcement, members of our team demonstrated our robot and showcased our work, highlighting the impact of STEM programs on students like us. These experiences have strengthened our understanding of real-world STEM applications and inspired us to continue growing as engineers and leaders.

Partnerships & Sponsorships

The Steel Titans actively build partnerships that support both our team and the broader community. We held a cookie fundraiser, where all proceeds were donated to the BELL Organization to support STEM-focused after-school programs for underserved youth in the Bronx. Through this initiative, we aimed not only to raise funds, but also to give back and expand access to STEM opportunities for others. Following this collaboration, the BELL Organization generously supported our team by donating LEGO SPIKE Prime kits, which have enhanced our ability to learn, build, and innovate, strengthening our robotics program. Our Claremont International High School has also been instrumental in supporting us by helping with our budget, giving permission for fundraisers, and allowing us to showcase our robots at school events. Through partnerships like these, we continue to create meaningful connections that promote STEM education, community impact, and mutual support.

Mentorship and Support

Our team is supported by dedicated mentors who guide us both technically and professionally throughout our robotics journey. At the NYC FIRST STEM Center at the Andrew Freedman House, we are mentored by Mr. Shorer and Mr. Rodriguez. They work with us during weekly robotics classes, teaching us essential skills in design, building, and programming while encouraging teamwork, problem-solving, and innovation. We are also supported by Jessy Devonou, an alumnus of our team, who provides guidance in coding and helps us develop our engineering portfolio. As a former team member, he brings valuable experience and insight that strengthens our growth. The NYC FIRST STEM Center provides a unique environment for students to explore STEM disciplines through hands-on learning. It is part of the broader FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) network, which inspires young people to become science and technology leaders. The center offers access to advanced tools, collaborative workspaces, and mentorship opportunities that help students grow both technically and personally. Located in the historic Andrew Freedman House, the STEM Center blends a rich cultural legacy with cutting-edge educational programs, giving students a creative space to innovate and collaborate on robotics, coding, and engineering projects. Through the support of our mentors and the resources provided by NYC FIRST and the AFH STEM Center, we continue to build not only our robot, but also our confidence, technical abilities, and leadership skills. This guidance fosters a strong foundation for our team to succeed in competitions, develop innovative solutions, and inspire the next generation of STEM leaders.

Collaboration with FTC teams

The Steel Titans actively collaborate with other FTC teams both in person and online to share knowledge, solve challenges, and grow together. This reflects the core value of FIRST: Gracious Professionalism. Many of the teams we work with have more experience than us, and their support has helped accelerate our learning. We regularly collaborate with 2 Train Robotics (FTC #24096) at the NYC FIRST STEM Center, where they assisted us with Mecanum drive coding. We also exchange parts and share design ideas. Kenny Donuts (FTC #20712) has supported us with troubleshooting and programming, while also collaborating with us by sharing materials and resources. Team Saturn (FTC #26195), last year’s Mayor’s Cup winner and this year’s FTC Decode Qualifier 2 Final Alliance winner, provided valuable suggestions to improve our robot’s shooter system. Team Mechatronics (FTC #13368) gave us feedback on scoring multiple game elements efficiently and shared ideas to strengthen team motivation and performance. We also participated in an FTC International Collaboration Meeting hosted by Couch Potatoes (FTC #24426), where 12 teams from 10 different countries met virtually to exchange experiences and strategies. Through these collaborations, we gained valuable technical knowledge in robot design, building, and competition strategy. We also developed new outreach ideas and strengthened our ability to work as part of a global STEM community.
